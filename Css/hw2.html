[2] His father was a teacher in a primary school, so everyone called him Munshiji. Later he took up the job of a clerk in the Revenue Department. Lal Bahadur's mother's name was Ramdulari. Being the youngest in the family, Lal Bahadur was fondly called Nanhe by his family members. Unfortunately, when Nanhe was eighteen months old, his father passed away. His mother Ramdulari went to her father Hazarilal's house in Mirzapur . After some time, his maternal grandfather also passed away. His maternal uncle Raghunath Prasad helped his mother a lot in raising Nanhe without a father . While living at his maternal grandparents' house, he received his primary education. After receiving the title of Shastri from Kashi Vidyapeeth, he removed the caste word 'Srivastava', which he had been using since birth, forever and added 'Shastri' to his name.
